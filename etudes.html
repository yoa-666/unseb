Classroom Management (PCG 1503)
EXAMEN TYPE : TRUE/FALSE · QUESTIONS OUVERTES · TOPIC
▼

Key definitions

TermDefinition
Classroom ManagementAll the techniques a teacher uses to take full control of the classroom and create a productive learning environment.
LockstepThe class grouping situation where all students are working with the teacher at the same time (whole-class activity).
Emergency PackageA set of extra activities the teacher uses to bring back students' attention or to warm up the class when there is spare time.
RulesStated expectations about students' behaviour in the classroom. They are set up at the first contact between the teacher and students.
ProceduresNon-stated expectations; they are established according to each case of disruptive behaviour. Both students and teacher are participants.
Gentle correctionShowing that incorrectness has occurred WITHOUT making a big fuss about it.

True/False — Reponses cles (examens recurrents)

StatementAnswer
Classroom Management is one of the most feared parts of teaching for new teachers.TRUE
Classroom Management can cause unhappiness and stress and lead individuals to leave teaching.TRUE
The first thing a teacher should do with a new class is to give a lesson.FALSE (Code of Conduct first)
The teacher should organize students in group randomly.FALSE
A code of conduct involves only the students.FALSE (students AND teacher)
Rules and Procedures are set up at the end of the semester.FALSE (at the beginning)
The teacher should use emergency package at the end of the lesson.FALSE (beginning)
The highest size of a regular classroom in Benin is 87.FALSE
Corporal punishment is the best way to keep learners disciplined today.FALSE
Gentle correction involves showing incorrectness and making a big fuss.FALSE
Lockstep is the traditional teaching situation.TRUE

3 main aspects of the physical environment

1. Classroom size (space available for activities)   2. Seating arrangement (rows, circles, groups)   3. Classroom resources (materials, equipment available)

Causes of discipline problems

Two sources : (1) The students (boredom, emotional issues, lack of motivation)  |  (2) The institutions (poor infrastructure, timetabling, early morning classes).

Teacher roles (Harmer)

Assessor — evaluates students' performance  |  Controller — totally in charge, manages the class  |  Prompter — makes suggestions when students are confused or silent  |  Resource — makes herself available to answer questions while students work  |  Tutor — checks and corrects during fluency activities  |  Role model — inspires by example  |  Participant — joins in activities  |  Observer — watches and listens

3 essential dimensions of teaching

Academic dimension (content knowledge)  |  Human dimension (interpersonal skills)  |  Pedagogical dimension (teaching strategies)  — Most important: Pedagogical, because it concerns the strategies and theoretical basis of teaching.

Astuce examen : Le sujet (topic/essay) demande souvent de commenter le role du professeur comme "role model" ou "resource provider". Pas plus de 10 lignes. Toujours mentionner la dimension de l'enseignement concernee.
Learning/Teaching Theories (TPE 1502)
EXAMEN TYPE : QCM · TRUE/FALSE · QUESTIONS OUVERTES
▼

Methods and their key features

MethodKey FeaturesTeacher role
GTM (Grammar-Translation)Goal: read literature in L2. Translation. Deductive grammar. No target language in class.Authority
Direct MethodOnly target language. Inductive grammar. Self-correction. Oral priority.Facilitator
AudiolingualEstablished during WWII. Habit formation. Drills. No deductive method. Mechanical.Authority
Silent WayTeacher mostly silent. Students discover. No homework. Cuisenaire rods.Silent observer
TPR (Total Physical Response)Commands + actions. Reduces anxiety. Focuses on listening, not writing. By James Asher.Commander
CLT (Communicative Language Teaching)Communication is the goal. Teacher = partner. Task-based.Partner
SuggestopediaNo homework. Relaxed atmosphere. Music. By Georgi Lozanov.Authority
CBA (Competency-Based Approach)Focus on competence demonstration. Teacher = tutor/facilitator/assessor.Facilitator

3 views of language learning

Structural view — language as a system of structural elements  |  Functional view — language as a vehicle for expression of functional meaning (semantic & communicative aspects)  |  Interactional view — language as a tool for creating and maintaining social relations

Classical Conditioning (Pavlov) vs Operant Conditioning (Skinner)

Classical : Stimulus → automatic response. Implication en classe : Teacher viewed as unconditioned stimulus.  |  Operant : Behavior reinforced by reward or punishment. Implication : Positive reinforcement encourages good behavior.

Contiguity rule (Behaviorists)

Goal: ensure that a stimulus and a response occur as closely together in time as possible so that the association is formed.

Benjamin Franklin's premises (Silent Way)

"Tell me and I forget" / "Teach me and I remember" / "Involve me and I learn" — These support the CBA programs because they emphasize learner involvement and active participation.

Deductive vs Inductive treatment of grammar

Deductive : Rule is given first, then examples and exercises.  |  Inductive : Learners discover the rule from context/reading/listening without being told it explicitly.

Astuce examen : Savoir associer chaque auteur a sa methode : Pavlov → Classical conditioning | Skinner → Operant conditioning | Gouin → Series method | Lozanov → Suggestopedia | James Asher → TPR | Chomsky → Language is not a habit structure (competence/performance).
Language Acquisition (PML 1501)
EXAMEN TYPE : QUESTIONS OUVERTES · QCM
▼

Key definitions

TermDefinition
Language AcquisitionThe unconscious process by which a person naturally develops the ability to perceive, produce and use language to communicate.
ESL (English as a Second Language)English learned in a country where English is the dominant/official language (e.g. Nigeria, USA).
EFL (English as a Foreign Language)English learned in a country where English is NOT the official language (e.g. Benin, France).
Positive TransferWhen L1 knowledge helps in learning L2 (similar structures facilitate learning).
Negative TransferWhen L1 knowledge interferes with L2 learning (causes errors).
InputThe language a learner is exposed to — it must be comprehensible (Krashen: i+1).
ZPDZone of Proximal Development (Vygotsky) — what a learner can do with help but not alone.
LADLanguage Acquisition Device (Chomsky) — innate mechanism that enables children to acquire language.
Mother TongueThe first language learned naturally by a child from birth.

3 theories of language acquisition

1. Behaviorist theory (Skinner) — habit formation through imitation and reinforcement  |  2. Nativist / Innatist theory (Chomsky) — humans are born with a LAD; language not a habit structure  |  3. Cognitive / Constructivist theory (Piaget, Vygotsky) — language develops through cognitive and social interaction

What happens when a child is never exposed to language?

Critical period ends without language acquisition. The child will find it extremely difficult or impossible to develop normal language skills later (Feral children studies, e.g. Genie case).

Astuce examen : Bien distinguer "Acquiring a language" (unconscious, natural) from "Learning a language" (conscious, formal instruction in a classroom).
Research Methodology (MRS 1504 / DQQ 1506 / AMS 1507)
EXAMEN TYPE : QCM · TRUE/FALSE · TABLE COMPARATIVE
▼

Quantitative vs Qualitative Research

Key PointsQuantitativeQualitative
Philosophical RootsPositivismConstructivism, Interpretivism
FocusQuantity, frequency, magnitudeMeaning, experience
GoalsConfirm/test hypothesesUnderstand, describe, discover
DesignStructured and predeterminedFlexible, emergent
Data collectionQuestionnaires, tests, numbersInterviews, observations, narratives
Researcher roleExternal, objectiveMain instrument (internal)

Mixed methods research key points

Uses BOTH qualitative and quantitative paradigms in one study. Advantages: overcomes limitations of a single design, helps generalize qualitative data, complements strengths. Designed = multiple phases. NOT useful when unexpected results arise from a prior study (FALSE). NOT time saving (FALSE).

7 parts of a research report (dissertation)

Introduction → Literature Review → Research Methods → Results → Discussion → Conclusion → References

Key definitions

TermDefinition
VariableSomething that can vary/change in a study.
ConstantSomething that does NOT vary.
HypothesisA testable prediction about the relationship between variables.
SamplingThe process of selecting participants to represent a larger population.
ReliabilityMeasures are stable on different occasions (consistent results).
ValidityThe study measures what it is supposed to measure.
Deductive theoryInvolves testing an explicitly defined hypothesis (hypothetico-deductive).
Reading & Writing Skills in EFL (ELR 1603)
EXAMEN TYPE : TRUE/FALSE · QUESTIONS OUVERTES
▼

Reading

TermDefinition
SkimmingReading fast to get an overall overview of the text (gist).
ScanningReading in search of specific information in the text.
Intensive readingReading shorter texts to extract specific information.
Extensive readingReading longer texts for one's own pleasure. Not for specific detail.
InferenceWorking out the meaning of something that is not explicitly stated in the text.
TranscriptA written version of a spoken text.
Bottom-upProcessing text by decoding individual words/letters first, then building up meaning.
Top-downUsing background knowledge and context to predict meaning before reading word by word.

Reading stages: Pre-reading (activate prior knowledge) → While-reading (comprehension tasks) → Post-reading (extension, discussion).

Reading is considered the third of the four language skills (Listening, Speaking, Reading, Writing).

Writing

Scaffolding — Temporary support given by the teacher to help learners complete tasks they cannot yet do alone. Gradually removed as learners become independent.

Process Approach (Kroll) — Writing as a recursive process: Prewriting → Drafting → Revising → Editing. Focus on the process, not just the product.

Two approaches: Product-oriented (focus on the final text, model to imitate) vs Process-oriented (focus on drafting and revision stages).

Testing & Evaluation (TEA 1601)
EXAMEN TYPE : QCM · TRUE/FALSE · BLOOM'S TAXONOMY MATCHING
▼

Key distinctions

TermDefinition
AssessmentJudgement of student performance compared to their peers or objectives. Ongoing.
EvaluationSystematic process of collecting and analysing data to make decisions. Broader than assessment.
TestingA specific instrument/tool used to collect data about students' knowledge.
Diagnostic testDone BEFORE learning to identify strengths and weaknesses.
Formative testDone DURING learning to monitor progress.
Summative testDone AFTER learning to evaluate what has been learned.
Certificative testFor official certification (BAC, BEPC...).
Proficiency testMeasures overall language ability, not tied to a course.
Placement testUsed to place students in the right level/group.
ValidityA test measures what it is supposed to measure.
ReliabilityA test gives consistent results across administrations.

Bloom's Taxonomy (6 levels)

LevelDefinitionVerbs
1. KnowledgeRecalling/remembering without necessarily understandingdefine, list, recall, identify
2. ComprehensionUnderstanding communicated informationexplain, paraphrase, summarize
3. ApplicationUsing general concept in a particular situationapply, solve, construct
4. AnalysisBreaking down into parts, identifying relationshipsanalyze, compare, contrast
5. SynthesisCreating something new from parts of different ideascompose, design, create
6. EvaluationJudging the value based on criteriaassess, evaluate, judge
Astuce examen : Dans les examens de Benin, pour recruter des enseignants le gouvernement utilise un test de type "proficiency test". Assessment ne signifie PAS la meme chose qu'evaluation (assessment comes BEFORE evaluation in the process). Good tests must be : valid, reliable AND practical.

QCM Interactif — Anglais L3

30 questionsBases sur les epreuves reelles
Q1What is "Lockstep"?PCG 1503
Lockstep is the traditional teaching situation where ALL students work with the teacher at the same time (whole-class). It is the opposite of pair work or group work.
Q2Rules and Procedures should be set up...PCG 1503
Rules and Procedures are set up at the very FIRST contact of the teacher and the students, NOT at the end of the semester. (Key exam answer: statement 5 = FALSE.)
Q3The teacher checks and corrects students during a fluency activity (where their conversation is faltering). This describes the role of...PCG 1503 — 2023-2024
Tutor — the teacher checks and corrects students ALONG during a fluency activity where their conversation is faltering (slightly). Answer: v - tutor (from the 2023-2024 exam).
Q4What are the THREE main aspects of the physical environment teachers should consider for classroom management?PCG 1503 — recurrent
The three main aspects of the physical environment are: (1) Classroom size, (2) Seating arrangement, (3) Classroom resources/equipment. This appears in multiple exams.
Q5The three essential dimensions of teaching are...PCG 1503 — 2017-2018
Academic dimension, Human dimension, and Pedagogical dimension. The most important is the PEDAGOGICAL dimension because it concerns the strategies and theoretical basis of teaching.
Q6The Audiolingual Method was established during...TPE 1502
The Audiolingual Method was established during World War II. This is a recurrent TRUE answer in the True/False section.
Q7In which method does the target language EXCLUSIVELY serve as the medium of instruction?TPE 1502 — Sem. 5 Janv. 2022
The DIRECT METHOD uses exclusively the target language to teach. No mother tongue is used at all. Answer: A - Direct Method. (Confirmed by multiple exam versions.)
Q8For Chomsky, language is...TPE 1502
For Chomsky, language is NOT a habit structure. Chomsky opposes Behaviorist theories. He believes language is an innate cognitive ability (LAD), not habits formed by repetition.
Q9TPR focuses attention on...TPE 1502 — True/False
TPR (Total Physical Response) focuses on LISTENING. Students respond to commands with physical actions. The teacher speaks, students act. Writing is NOT the focus. (Exam statement: "TPR focuses attention to Reading and Writing" = RIGHT/TRUE... wait: FALSE — it focuses on LISTENING).
Q10Which method uses a DEDUCTIVE application of grammar rules?TPE 1502 — Janv. 2022
Grammar-Translation Method (GTM) uses a DEDUCTIVE approach: the rule is given first, then examples and exercises. Direct Method uses INDUCTIVE (students discover rules).
Q11In the CBA context, the main role of the teacher is...TPE 1502 — 2019-2020
In the CBA (Competency-Based Approach), the teacher plays the role of FACILITATOR (also called tutor or coach). The learner is at the center. Answer: d - coach/facilitator.
Q12ESL (English as a Second Language) is learned...PML 1501
ESL = learned where English IS the official language (immersion context). EFL = learned where English is NOT the official language (e.g. Benin = EFL context).
Q13ZPD stands for...PML 1501
ZPD = Zone of Proximal Development (Vygotsky). It refers to what a learner can do WITH assistance but not yet independently. LAD = Language Acquisition Device (Chomsky).
Q14Negative Transfer in language learning refers to...PML 1501
Negative Transfer = L1 INTERFERES with L2 (causes errors). Positive Transfer = L1 HELPS in learning L2. Example of negative transfer: French speaker says "I am agree" because in French "Je suis d'accord" uses "etre".
Q15For Chomsky, children are able to acquire language because of an innate mechanism called...PML 1501
LAD = Language Acquisition Device. According to Chomsky's Nativist theory, all humans are born with an innate LAD that allows them to acquire language. This is why children can learn any language they are exposed to.
Q16Which research paradigm is least concerned about generalizing its findings?AMS 1507 — Dec. 2019
QUALITATIVE research is least concerned about generalizing findings. It focuses on deep understanding of a specific context, not on producing generalizable statistical results. Answer: b - qualitative research.
Q17In qualitative research, the researcher is...DQQ 1506
In qualitative research, the researcher IS the main instrument. They observe, interpret and analyze without needing external measurement tools. TRUE in multiple exam True/False sections.
Q18The abstract of a dissertation should be about how many words?MRS 1504 — 2019-2020
The abstract should be about 300 words. Answer: c-300. The abstract is a concise summary of the entire study (purpose, methods, findings, conclusions).
Q19The process of selecting participants to represent a larger group is known as...MRS 1504
Sampling = selecting participants who represent a larger population. Answer: c - Sampling. This is a recurrent question in the MCQ section of MRS 1504.
Q20Both quantitative and qualitative investigation CAN be used in the same research. This statement is...DQQ 1506 — True/False
TRUE — When both paradigms are used in the same study, it is called MIXED METHODS RESEARCH. Exam answer: "Both quantitative and qualitative investigation cannot be used in the same research" = FALSE.
Q21What does "Scaffolding" in Writing mean?ELR 1603
Scaffolding = TEMPORARY support given to learners to help them complete a task they cannot yet do alone. Once they are independent, the scaffold is removed. This concept is linked to Vygotsky's ZPD.
Q22Reading is considered the _____ of the four language skills.ELR 1603 — True/False
Reading is considered the THIRD skill. The order is: 1. Listening, 2. Speaking, 3. Reading, 4. Writing. Exam statement: "Reading is considered to be the second of the four language skills" = FALSE.
Q23Skimming is defined as...ELR 1603 — 2017-2018
Skimming = reading FAST to get the general idea / overview of the text (gist). Scanning = reading to find SPECIFIC information. Often confused: skimming is the "big picture", scanning is looking for one detail.
Q24The "Process Approach" in Writing focuses on...ELR 1603
Process Approach = focuses on the STAGES of writing (recursive: prewriting → drafting → revising → editing), NOT just the final product. Kroll emphasizes writing as a recursive process.
Q25Assessment and Evaluation mean the same thing. This statement is...TEA 1601 — recurrent
FALSE. Assessment and Evaluation are NOT the same. Assessment is the judgment of student performance (ongoing), while Evaluation is the systematic process of collecting and analysing data to make broader decisions. Assessment comes before evaluation in the process.
Q26A test is said to be VALID when...TEA 1601
VALIDITY = a test measures what it is supposed to measure and NOTHING ELSE. RELIABILITY = consistent results. PRACTICALITY = easy to administer. All three qualities are needed for a GOOD test.
Q27In Bloom's Taxonomy, "Synthesis" involves...TEA 1601 — Bloom's matching
Synthesis (level 5) = creating something new from parts. Verbs: compose, design, create, formulate. NOT to be confused with Evaluation (judging value) or Application (using a concept in new situations).
Q28To recruit teachers in Benin today, the government submits candidates to which type of test?TEA 1601 — True/False
Proficiency test — the Benin government uses a PROFICIENCY TEST to recruit teachers (CAPES/concours). A proficiency test measures overall language ability regardless of any course. Exam answer: "To recruit part-time teachers, the government went through formative test" = FALSE.
Q29An appraisal addressed to a student to encourage and motivate them is...TEA 1601 — QCM 2019-2020
An appraisal to ENCOURAGE and MOTIVATE is an ASSESSMENT (not an evaluation, which is broader and more systematic). Answer: b - assessment.
Q30The main focus of Competency-Based Education (CBE) is...TPE 1502 — Fevrier 2024
The main focus of CBE = THE DEMONSTRATION OF COMPETENCE (not time, not content). Students progress when they CAN DO something, not when they have sat in class long enough. Answer: c.

Indices de diversité floristique

Indice de Shannon : H = −∑ Pi × log⊂2; Pi   (avec Pi = ni/N)
Indice de Jaccard : IJ = C/(A+B) × 100   (C = esp. communes, A+B = totaux)
Indice de Sorensen : IS = (C × 100) × 2/(A+B)
Si IJ ≥ 50% : les groupements 1 et 2 sont similaires
Biomes : biome terrestre (forêts, steppes, déserts) + biome aquatique (toundra ex.). • Unité taxonomique fondamentale = l'espèce. • Rang végétal : Embranchement → Classe → Ordre → Famille → Genre → espèce.
